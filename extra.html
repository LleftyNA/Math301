Fundamental theorem of pyraminx

Orientation:

We represent a configuration by
A permutation of the 4 center blocks
A permutation of the 6 edge blocks
An orientation vector of the 4 center blocks
An orientation vector of the 6 center blocks
Then, marking the primary facet of blocks. For an edge block, mark the sticker with a 0/+ if it is in the primary facet, and mark the other stricker on the same block with a 1. For center block, I mark 0/+, 1, 2 from clockwise direction around the block.

Vector:

Let the position vector be 4-tuple ( ρ , σ , v , w ).

Notations and vectors:

There are four basic notation moves, F U R L. And inverse.
The permutation and vectors are showed in the table below:

Notations ρ σ v w
Right clockwise e (4,5,6) (0,0,0,2) (0,0,0,0,1,1)
Left clockwise e (1,2,6) (0,2,0,0) (1,1,0,0,0,0)
Upper clockwise e (1,5,3) (2,0,0,0) (0,0,1,0,1,0)
Right clockwise e (2,3,4) (0,0,2,0) (0,1,0,0,1,0)

For inverse of it, the parity of permutations and edge-vectors stay the same.
However, the center block vector can twist by it own, so it can be 1.
For example, R' , v is (0,0,0,1). Same for other notations.

Conclusion:

a: Parity for permutations.
b: Because of 2k + 1 can be odd or even, that means very single center move is possible.
c: Sum need to be 0(mod 2).
